# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Policy Owner:** People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2025-10-01  
**Effective Date:** 2022-01-01  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Applies To:** All full-time team members globally  

---

## 1. Philosophy

We believe that rest is not a reward — it is a prerequisite for sustained high performance. Acme Corp operates on a **flexible PTO** model. There is no fixed number of vacation days. Instead, we trust team members to take the time they need to recharge, handle personal matters, and maintain their well-being.

Flexible PTO does not mean "unlimited but actually zero." We set clear minimum expectations and track usage to ensure people are genuinely taking time off.

## 2. PTO Guidelines

### 2.1 Minimum PTO Requirement

Every full-time team member is expected to take a **minimum of 15 business days** of PTO per calendar year. This minimum is non-negotiable. Managers are responsible for ensuring their direct reports meet this threshold.

If a team member has taken fewer than 8 days of PTO by July 1, their manager will initiate a check-in conversation to plan remaining time off.

### 2.2 Maximum Consecutive Days

Team members may take up to **25 consecutive business days** (5 weeks) of PTO. Requests exceeding 25 consecutive days require VP-level approval and a written coverage plan.

### 2.3 Advance Notice

| Duration | Notice Required |
|---|---|
| 1-2 days | 3 business days |
| 3-5 days | 2 weeks |
| 6-10 days | 4 weeks |
| 11+ days | 6 weeks |

Emergency situations (illness, family emergency, bereavement) are exempt from notice requirements.

### 2.4 Requesting PTO

1. Discuss with your manager informally to confirm coverage
2. Submit a request in BambooHR with the dates and type of leave
3. Block the time on your Google Calendar as "Out of Office"
4. Set an auto-reply on email and update your Slack status
5. Post in your team's Slack channel so colleagues are aware

Managers should approve or deny PTO requests within 2 business days. Denial must include a reason and an alternative timeframe.

### 2.5 PTO During Critical Periods

Some teams have known critical periods (e.g., end-of-quarter for Sales, release weeks for Engineering). Team leads should publish a list of blackout dates at the start of each quarter. PTO during blackout dates is not automatically denied but requires explicit discussion with your manager.

## 3. Types of Leave

### 3.1 Vacation

Standard time off for rest, travel, or personal activities. No reason needs to be provided.

### 3.2 Sick Leave

Time off for physical or mental illness. No doctor's note is required for absences of 5 days or fewer. For absences exceeding 5 consecutive days, a doctor's note may be requested — this is for duty-of-care purposes, not surveillance.

Sick leave does not count against PTO tracking for minimum-usage purposes.

### 3.3 Bereavement Leave

Team members receive paid bereavement leave as follows:

| Relationship | Days |
|---|---|
| Spouse/domestic partner | 10 days |
| Child, parent, sibling | 10 days |
| Grandparent, grandchild | 5 days |
| In-law, aunt, uncle, cousin | 3 days |
| Close friend | 3 days |

Additional time may be taken as PTO or unpaid leave. Managers have discretion to extend bereavement leave on a case-by-case basis.

### 3.4 Parental Leave

Acme Corp provides **16 weeks of fully paid parental leave** for all new parents, regardless of gender or method of becoming a parent (birth, adoption, surrogacy, foster placement). Key details:

- Leave may be taken in one continuous block or split into two blocks within the first 12 months
- An additional 4 weeks of part-time (50%) return-to-work transition is available
- Parental leave runs concurrently with any statutory leave (FMLA, local equivalents)
- Both parents are eligible for the full 16 weeks if both work at Acme Corp

### 3.5 Jury Duty

Time off for jury service is fully paid. Provide your manager with the jury summons as soon as you receive it.

### 3.6 Voting Leave

Team members receive up to 4 hours of paid leave on election days to vote. This applies to national and local elections in your country of residence.

### 3.7 Military Leave

We comply with all applicable laws regarding military service leave (USERRA in the United States, equivalent laws elsewhere). Contact People Operations for details specific to your jurisdiction.

### 3.8 Sabbatical

After 4 years of continuous employment, team members are eligible for a **4-week paid sabbatical** in addition to regular PTO. The sabbatical resets every 4 years. It must be taken as a single continuous block.

Planning requirements:
- Notify your manager at least 3 months in advance
- Create a written coverage plan for your responsibilities
- Designate a point of contact for urgent matters
- Fully disconnect during the sabbatical — no Slack, no email, no "quick check-ins"

### 3.9 Unpaid Leave

Unpaid leave of up to 30 days may be requested for personal reasons not covered by other leave categories. Requires manager and VP approval. Benefits coverage continues during unpaid leave up to 30 days.

## 4. Public Holidays

### 4.1 Company-Wide Holidays

Acme Corp observes the following company-wide days off regardless of location:

| Date | Holiday |
|---|---|
| January 1 | New Year's Day |
| December 25 | Winter Holiday |
| December 31 | Year-End Rest Day |

### 4.2 Location-Specific Holidays

In addition to company-wide holidays, team members observe the public holidays of their country of residence. Common examples:

- **United States:** MLK Day, Presidents Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day (Jul 4), Labor Day, Thanksgiving (2 days)
- **United Kingdom:** Easter Monday, Early May Bank Holiday, Spring Bank Holiday, Summer Bank Holiday, Boxing Day
- **Germany:** Easter Friday/Monday, Tag der Arbeit (May 1), Tag der Deutschen Einheit (Oct 3)
- **India:** Republic Day (Jan 26), Holi, Independence Day (Aug 15), Diwali, Gandhi Jayanti

Team members should enter their local holidays in BambooHR at the start of each year. If a local holiday falls on a weekend, the next business day is observed.

### 4.3 Family and Friends Day

Four times per year, the company declares a "Family and Friends Day" — an additional day off for everyone. These are announced at the start of each quarter, typically on a Friday adjacent to existing holidays.

## 5. PTO and Performance

### 5.1 Taking PTO Is Expected

PTO usage is never a negative factor in performance reviews. In fact, consistently failing to take PTO is a concern that managers are expected to address.

### 5.2 Coverage Planning

Before taking PTO of 3 or more days:

1. Document the status of your active projects
2. Assign a backup for any time-sensitive responsibilities
3. Set up auto-replies that redirect inquiries to the appropriate person
4. Update any on-call schedules

### 5.3 Returning from PTO

After returning from PTO of 5 or more days:

- Spend the first half-day catching up on messages and context
- Do not schedule meetings on your first day back
- Your manager should provide a brief written summary of anything critical that happened while you were out

## 6. Tracking and Reporting

### 6.1 How We Track PTO

All PTO is tracked in BambooHR. Team members are responsible for logging their time off accurately.

### 6.2 Manager Dashboard

Managers have access to a PTO dashboard that shows:

- Days taken per team member (YTD)
- Upcoming scheduled PTO
- Team members below the minimum pace
- Team coverage calendar

### 6.3 Company-Level Reporting

People Operations publishes a quarterly PTO report including:

- Average PTO days taken per team member
- Distribution by department and region
- Percentage of team members on track for the 15-day minimum
- Trends compared to prior quarters

## 7. PTO at Separation

### 7.1 Voluntary Resignation

Since PTO is flexible (not accrued), there is no payout of unused PTO upon resignation. Team members are encouraged to use their PTO before their last day.

### 7.2 Involuntary Termination

Similarly, no PTO payout applies. If local law requires payout of accrued vacation, we comply with the statutory minimum.

### 7.3 Accrued Statutory Leave

In jurisdictions where the law mandates accrued leave (e.g., most EU countries, Australia), the statutory minimum is tracked separately and paid out upon separation as required by law.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is there truly no cap on PTO?**  
A: There is no annual cap, but there is a 25 consecutive business day limit. If you want to take a 3-month trip, talk to your manager about an extended leave arrangement.

**Q: Can I work from a different country during PTO?**  
A: PTO means you are not working. If you want to work from a different location, that is a temporary relocation, not PTO. See the Remote Work Policy.

**Q: What if my manager pressures me not to take PTO?**  
A: This violates our policy. Report it to People Operations or use the anonymous reporting hotline.

**Q: Does PTO carry over?**  
A: Since PTO is not accrued, there is nothing to carry over. Every year starts fresh with the same expectation: take at least 15 days.

**Q: Can I donate PTO to a colleague?**  
A: Since PTO is flexible and not accrued, there is no bank to donate from. If a colleague needs extended leave, they should work with People Operations on an accommodation.

## 9. Regional Addenda

### 9.1 European Union

For team members employed in the EU, the following additional provisions apply per the EU Working Time Directive:

- Minimum 4 weeks (20 working days) of paid annual leave guaranteed by law
- This statutory minimum is tracked separately in BambooHR
- Any unused statutory leave must be taken or paid out per local law
- Sick leave provisions follow local statutory requirements, which may differ from this policy

### 9.2 United States

For US-based team members:

- Flexible PTO satisfies all state-level vacation requirements
- California, Colorado, Illinois, and other states with specific PTO laws: statutory minimums are tracked separately
- FMLA-eligible leave runs concurrently with company leave policies

### 9.3 Canada

- Provincial employment standards minimums are tracked separately
- Quebec: statutory 3 weeks after 3 years of service
- All provinces: statutory holidays observed per provincial calendar
