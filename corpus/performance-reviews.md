# Performance Review Process

**Policy Owner:** People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-01  
**Effective Date:** 2023-04-01  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Applies To:** All full-time team members  

---

## 1. Philosophy

Performance reviews at Acme Corp exist to achieve three things:

1. **Alignment:** Ensure every team member understands how their work connects to company goals
2. **Growth:** Identify strengths to leverage and areas to develop
3. **Fairness:** Make compensation and promotion decisions based on consistent, documented criteria

Reviews are not a surprise. If you learn something negative about your performance for the first time during a review, your manager has failed — not you.

## 2. Review Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Formal performance review | Semi-annual | April and October |
| Manager-direct report 1:1 | Weekly | Ongoing |

| Promotion review | Semi-annual | Aligned with April and October reviews |
| Probationary review (new hires) | End of month 3 and month 6 | From start date |

## 3. Performance Dimensions

Every team member is evaluated on four dimensions. The weight of each dimension varies by role level.

### 3.1 Results

Did you deliver on your commitments? This is measured against:

- OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) set at the beginning of each quarter
- Project deliverables and deadlines
- Quantitative metrics relevant to your role (e.g., uptime for SREs, pipeline generated for SDRs)

### 3.2 Collaboration

How effectively do you work with others? Indicators include:

- Quality of code reviews, document feedback, and cross-team contributions
- Responsiveness and reliability as a collaborator
- Handling of disagreements (constructive vs. destructive)
- Mentoring and knowledge sharing

### 3.3 Communication

How well do you communicate in a remote-first environment?

- Quality and timeliness of written communication
- Documentation contributions
- Proactive status updates and transparency about blockers
- Effectiveness in meetings (prepared, concise, action-oriented)

### 3.4 Values Alignment

Do you exemplify Acme Corp's values in your day-to-day work?

- **Transparency:** Shares information openly, documents decisions
- **Iteration:** Ships small improvements frequently rather than waiting for perfection
- **Efficiency:** Avoids unnecessary process, respects others' time
- **Diversity, Inclusion, and Belonging:** Creates an environment where everyone can contribute
- **Results:** Focuses on outcomes, not activity

### 3.5 Dimension Weights by Level

| Level | Results | Collaboration | Communication | Values |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Individual Contributor (IC1-IC3) | 40% | 25% | 20% | 15% |
| Senior IC (IC4-IC5) | 35% | 25% | 20% | 20% |
| Staff+ IC (IC6+) | 30% | 25% | 25% | 20% |
| Manager (M1-M2) | 30% | 25% | 25% | 20% |
| Director+ (M3+) | 25% | 25% | 25% | 25% |

## 4. Rating Scale

### 4.1 Performance Ratings

| Rating | Description | Typical Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Exceeds** | Consistently delivered above expectations across most dimensions. Impact well beyond role scope. | ~15% |
| **Meets+** | Met all expectations and exceeded in at least one dimension. Reliable high performer. | ~30% |
| **Meets** | Delivered on commitments. Performing at the level expected for the role. This is a good rating. | ~40% |
| **Developing** | Fell short in one or more dimensions. Specific areas for improvement identified. | ~12% |
| **Below** | Significant performance gaps. Performance improvement plan will be initiated. | ~3% |

### 4.2 Calibration

Ratings are not final until calibrated. Calibration ensures consistency across managers and teams.

**Calibration process:**

1. Managers submit proposed ratings with written justification
2. Directors review ratings within their org for consistency
3. VP-level calibration session: Directors present cases for Exceeds and Developing/Below ratings
4. People Operations reviews for bias patterns (gender, race, location, tenure)
5. Final ratings communicated to team members

Calibration sessions are completed within 2 weeks of the review submission deadline.

## 5. Review Process (Step by Step)

### 5.1 Timeline (April Cycle Example)

| Date | Activity | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| March 1 | Review cycle announced, self-review opens | People Ops |
| March 1-15 | Team members complete self-reviews | Team member |
| March 1-15 | Peer feedback requested (2-4 peers per person) | Team member + Manager |
| March 15-31 | Managers write reviews, incorporating self-review and peer feedback | Manager |
| April 1-7 | Calibration sessions | Directors + VPs |
| April 8-14 | Final ratings approved | VPs + People Ops |
| April 15-30 | Review conversations (manager delivers review to direct report) | Manager |
| May 1 | Reviews finalized in BambooHR | People Ops |

### 5.2 Self-Review

Every team member writes a self-review covering:

1. **Key accomplishments** this period (3-5 bullet points with specific outcomes and metrics)
2. **Challenges encountered** and how you addressed them
3. **Areas for growth** you've identified
4. **Feedback for your manager** on how they can better support you
5. **Career aspirations** for the next 6-12 months

Self-reviews should be 500-1,000 words. Longer is not better — be specific and concise.

### 5.3 Peer Feedback

Each team member selects 2-4 peers to provide feedback. Managers may add 1-2 additional peers to ensure diverse perspectives.

Peer feedback questions:

1. What is this person's greatest strength as a collaborator?
2. What is one specific area where they could improve?
3. Describe a situation where this person demonstrated strong (or weak) alignment with our values.
4. Any other comments?

Peer feedback is **confidential** — the reviewer's identity is not shared with the team member. Managers may share paraphrased themes but not attributable quotes.

### 5.4 Manager Review

Managers write a review for each direct report that includes:

1. **Overall rating** with justification
2. **Rating per dimension** (Results, Collaboration, Communication, Values)
3. **Specific examples** supporting each dimensional rating (minimum 2 per dimension)
4. **Strengths to leverage** (2-3 bullet points)
5. **Areas for development** (2-3 bullet points with concrete suggestions)
6. **Growth plan** for the next 6 months (aligned with the team member's career aspirations)

### 5.5 Review Conversation

The review conversation is a 60-minute meeting (one of the few times we schedule 60 minutes):

**Structure:**

- **First 10 minutes:** Manager shares the overall assessment and rating
- **Next 20 minutes:** Discussion of each dimension — strengths and development areas
- **Next 15 minutes:** Team member shares their perspective, asks questions, pushes back
- **Final 15 minutes:** Co-create the growth plan for the next 6 months

**Ground rules:**

- The manager shares the written review at least 24 hours before the conversation so the team member can prepare
- No surprises — anything in the review should have been discussed in prior 1:1s
- The team member may disagree with the rating. Disagreement is documented and escalated to the director if unresolved.
- The conversation is documented in BambooHR within 3 business days

## 6. Promotions

### 6.1 Promotion Criteria

Promotions are based on **sustained performance at the next level**, not a reward for current-level performance. The team member must demonstrate:

1. Consistently performing the responsibilities of the target level for at least 2 quarters
2. Meeting or exceeding expectations in all four performance dimensions
3. Positive peer and cross-functional feedback
4. Manager sponsorship with a written promotion case

### 6.2 Promotion Process

1. Manager identifies a direct report ready for promotion
2. Manager writes a promotion document with evidence against the target level's expectations
3. Promotion is discussed during calibration
4. Director and VP approve
5. People Operations validates against compensation bands and headcount
6. Promotion is communicated to the team member
7. Announced in #announcements (with the team member's consent)

### 6.3 Promotion Frequency

- Team members are eligible for promotion review at every semi-annual cycle
- There is no minimum tenure requirement at a level
- Back-to-back promotions (two cycles in a row) require VP justification and are rare
- Skip-level promotions (e.g., IC3 to IC5) are not permitted

### 6.4 Compensation Adjustment at Promotion

Upon promotion, compensation is adjusted to at least the minimum of the new level's band. Typical adjustments:

| Promotion Type | Typical Base Increase |
|---|---|
| Within-track (e.g., IC3 → IC4) | 10-15% |
| Cross-track (e.g., IC4 → M1) | 10-15% + management equity refresh |
| Senior promotions (IC5+ or M3+) | 12-20% |

Equity refresh grants are also evaluated at promotion.

## 7. Underperformance

### 7.1 Informal Coaching

Before any formal action, managers are expected to address performance concerns through regular 1:1s and direct feedback. Most performance issues are resolved at this stage.

Document coaching conversations in your 1:1 notes. Include:

- The specific concern
- The expected standard
- What support you're providing
- A check-in date to assess progress

### 7.2 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

If informal coaching does not resolve the concern within 4-6 weeks, a formal Performance Improvement Plan is initiated.

A PIP includes:

1. **Specific performance gaps** with examples
2. **Clear success criteria** — what "meeting expectations" looks like, with measurable outcomes
3. **Timeline:** 30 or 60 days depending on the nature of the gaps
4. **Support provided:** Additional 1:1s, training, mentorship, workload adjustment
5. **Weekly check-ins** between the team member and manager
6. **Possible outcomes:** Return to good standing, extension, or separation

**PIP statistics (2025):**

| Outcome | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Returned to good standing | 42% |
| Voluntary resignation during PIP | 31% |
| PIP extended | 8% |
| Terminated at end of PIP | 19% |

### 7.3 Separation

If a PIP does not result in the required improvement, separation is initiated. The separation process involves:

1. Manager recommendation with documentation
2. People Operations review
3. Legal review
4. VP approval
5. Separation conversation (conducted by manager with People Ops present)
6. Severance per the Separation Policy (typically 2 weeks per year of service, minimum 4 weeks)

## 8. Career Development Framework

### 8.1 Career Tracks

Acme Corp offers two parallel career tracks:

**Individual Contributor Track:**

| Level | Title | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| IC1 | Associate [Role] | Learning the role with guidance |
| IC2 | [Role] | Independent contributor on defined tasks |
| IC3 | Senior [Role] | Owns projects end-to-end, mentors IC1-IC2 |
| IC4 | Staff [Role] | Cross-team impact, sets technical direction |
| IC5 | Principal [Role] | Org-wide impact, recognized external expert |
| IC6 | Distinguished [Role] | Company-wide impact, industry thought leader |

**Management Track:**

| Level | Title | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| M1 | Manager | 4-8 direct reports, team-level ownership |
| M2 | Senior Manager | 8-15 direct reports or managers of ICs |
| M3 | Director | Owns a function or sub-department |
| M4 | Senior Director | Multiple functions, org design responsibility |
| M5 | VP | Department-level ownership, executive team |
| M6 | SVP / C-level | Company-wide strategy |

### 8.2 Level Expectations

Detailed level expectations for each role family are published in the Career Framework section of the handbook. Each level description includes:

- Scope of impact (individual → team → org → company)
- Technical/functional skills expected
- Leadership and influence expectations
- Communication expectations
- Decision-making authority
- Example projects at this level

### 8.3 Switching Tracks

Team members may switch between IC and management tracks. This is neither a promotion nor a demotion — it is a lateral move with different responsibilities.

To switch tracks:

1. Discuss with your manager and skip-level manager
2. Identify an open role on the target track
3. Complete a modified interview process (focused on the new track's competencies)
4. If approved, a 90-day trial period begins with a designated mentor on the new track

## 9. Manager Responsibilities

Managers are the linchpin of the performance review process. Specific responsibilities:

- **Weekly 1:1s** with every direct report (30 minutes minimum, never cancelled two weeks in a row)
- **Continuous feedback** — no surprises at review time
- **Document performance** throughout the cycle (not just the last 2 weeks)
- **Calibrate fairly** — advocate for your team while being honest about gaps
- **Deliver reviews in person** (via video) — never by email or Slack
- **Follow through** on growth plans with concrete support

### 9.1 Manager Accountability

Manager effectiveness is measured through:

- Direct report engagement survey scores (quarterly pulse survey)
- Direct report retention rates
- Promotion rate of direct reports
- Quality of review documentation (assessed by People Operations)
- 360 feedback from direct reports (annual, anonymous)

Managers with consistently low engagement scores or high attrition undergo additional coaching. Persistent underperformance as a manager may result in a return to the IC track.

## 10. Bias Mitigation

### 10.1 Known Biases in Performance Reviews

We actively guard against:

- **Recency bias:** Overweighting recent events. Use running notes throughout the cycle.
- **Halo/horn effect:** One strong/weak dimension coloring the overall assessment. Rate each dimension independently.
- **Proximity bias:** Favoring team members in similar time zones or who are more visible in Slack. Use objective metrics.
- **Leniency/strictness bias:** Some managers rate higher/lower than peers. Calibration corrects for this.
- **Similarity bias:** Favoring people who are similar to you. Seek peer feedback from diverse colleagues.

### 10.2 Bias Checks

People Operations runs the following analyses after each review cycle:

- Rating distribution by gender, race/ethnicity, location, and tenure
- Promotion rates by demographic group
- Correlation between manager and rating distribution skew
- Comparison of self-review ratings vs. manager ratings by demographic group

Results are shared with the executive team and published internally (aggregated, not individual).

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I disagree with my rating?**  
A: Discuss it with your manager first. If unresolved, escalate to your skip-level manager. If still unresolved, contact People Operations for a formal review.

**Q: Can my rating change after calibration?**  
A: Yes, that's the point of calibration. Ratings may go up or down. Your manager will explain any changes.

**Q: How does PTO usage affect my review?**  
A: It doesn't. Taking PTO is expected and is never a negative factor.

**Q: What if I changed teams or managers mid-cycle?**  
A: Both your current and former manager contribute to the review. The current manager delivers it.

**Q: Are reviews tied to compensation?**  
A: Reviews inform compensation decisions but are not formulaic. The annual compensation review in November considers performance rating, market data, internal equity, and budget.

**Q: Can I see my peer feedback?**  
A: You see themes and paraphrased feedback. You do not see individual responses or who said what.
