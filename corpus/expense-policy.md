# Expense Policy

**Policy Owner:** Finance  
**Last Updated:** 2025-08-20  
**Effective Date:** 2022-07-01  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Applies To:** All team members and authorized contractors  

---

## 1. General Principles

Acme Corp reimburses reasonable business expenses incurred in the course of your work. We trust team members to exercise good judgment and spend company money as if it were their own.

The guiding rule: **Would you be comfortable if your expense report were posted in a public Slack channel?** If yes, submit it. If you hesitate, reconsider.

### 1.1 Key Rules

1. All expenses must have a **legitimate business purpose**
2. Receipts are required for any expense over **$25 USD**
3. Expenses must be submitted within **60 days** of being incurred
4. Expenses submitted after 60 days require VP-level approval and may be denied
5. Falsified expense reports are grounds for immediate termination
6. When in doubt, ask in #finance before spending

## 2. Expense Categories and Limits

### 2.1 Travel

#### Flights

| Flight Duration | Class | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Under 4 hours | Economy | Manager |
| 4-6 hours | Economy (premium economy if unavailable) | Manager |
| 6-10 hours | Premium Economy | Manager |
| Over 10 hours | Business Class | Director |
| Any duration | First Class | Not reimbursable |

Booking guidelines:
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Use the company travel portal (Navan) for all bookings — this ensures travel insurance coverage
- Select refundable fares only when the trip is uncertain; otherwise book the lowest reasonable fare
- Loyalty program preferences are fine as long as the fare difference is under 10%
- Seat upgrades using personal miles/points are permitted; cash upgrades are not reimbursable

#### Hotels

| Location Tier | Nightly Rate Cap |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 (SF, NYC, London, Tokyo, Zurich) | $300 |
| Tier 2 (Seattle, Austin, Berlin, Sydney, Toronto) | $250 |
| Tier 3 (All other locations) | $200 |

Guidelines:
- Book through Navan when possible
- Airbnb/VRBO is acceptable for stays of 5+ nights if the nightly rate is below the hotel cap
- Laundry services are reimbursable for stays exceeding 4 nights
- Mini-bar, in-room movies, and spa services are **not reimbursable**
- Room cancellation fees are reimbursable if the trip was cancelled for business reasons

#### Ground Transportation

- **Ride-sharing (Uber/Lyft):** Standard/economy tier. Rides over $50 require a note explaining why (e.g., airport transfer with luggage)
- **Rental cars:** Compact or midsize. Full-size or SUV requires justification (e.g., transporting equipment)
- **Parking:** Reimbursable at reasonable rates; long-term lot preferred over daily garage rates
- **Public transit:** Fully reimbursable
- **Personal vehicle mileage:** $0.67/mile (IRS 2025 rate) with origin/destination logged

#### Per Diem (Meals While Traveling)

| Meal | Limit |
|---|---|
| Breakfast | $20 |
| Lunch | $30 |
| Dinner | $50 |
| Daily total | $100 |

- Alcohol with meals is reimbursable up to a reasonable amount (1-2 drinks) — don't submit a $200 bar tab
- Room service follows the same per-meal limits
- Grocery purchases in lieu of restaurant meals are reimbursable (and encouraged for longer stays)
- Tips up to 20% are included in the meal limits

### 2.2 Client Entertainment

| Type | Per-Person Limit | Approval |
|---|---|---|
| Client meals | $100 | Manager |
| Client events (sports, concerts) | $250 | Director |
| Client gifts | $150 | Manager |

Requirements:
- Log the names of all attendees and the business purpose
- The ratio of Acme Corp attendees to clients should not exceed 1:1
- No gifts to government employees or officials
- Recurring entertainment with the same client requires quarterly review

### 2.3 Team Events

Managers may spend up to **$75 per person per quarter** on team social events. This covers:

- Virtual team activities (wine tastings, cooking classes, trivia)
- In-person team meals during offsites
- Celebration events (product launches, milestone achievements)

Team events exceeding the per-person limit require director approval.

## 3. Expense Submission Process

### 3.1 How to Submit

1. Log in to **Expensify** (SSO via Google)
2. Create a new expense report
3. Add each expense with:
   - Date of expense
   - Vendor/merchant name
   - Amount and currency
   - Category (from dropdown)
   - Business purpose (1-2 sentences)
   - Receipt image (photo or PDF)
4. Submit the report for approval

### 3.2 Approval Workflow

| Expense Amount | Approver |
|---|---|
| Under $500 total | Direct manager |
| $500-$2,500 | Direct manager + Finance review |
| $2,500-$10,000 | Director |
| Over $10,000 | VP + CFO |

Managers must approve or reject expense reports within **5 business days**.

### 3.3 Reimbursement Timeline

- Approved expenses are reimbursed in the next payroll cycle
- For US team members, this is typically within 2 weeks of approval
- International team members may experience an additional 1-2 weeks due to currency conversion and EOR processing
- Direct deposit required — no paper checks

### 3.4 Currency

- Submit expenses in the **currency in which they were incurred**
- Conversion to USD (or your home currency) is handled by Expensify at the exchange rate on the date of the expense
- If using a personal credit card, submit the amount charged to your card (including any foreign transaction fees)
- Company-issued virtual cards (Brex) should be used for recurring expenses and purchases over $500

### 3.5 Lost Receipts

If you lose a receipt:

1. Attempt to obtain a duplicate from the vendor
2. If unavailable, complete the "Missing Receipt Affidavit" in Expensify
3. Missing receipt affidavits are limited to **3 per quarter**
4. Expenses over $75 without receipts may be denied

## 4. Company Credit Cards

### 4.1 Brex Virtual Cards

All team members have access to Brex virtual cards for business expenses. Virtual cards can be generated instantly through the Brex dashboard.

- Use virtual cards for online purchases, subscriptions, and vendor payments
- Each virtual card can have a custom spending limit
- Transactions appear automatically in Expensify for reconciliation
- You still need to add receipts and business purpose in Expensify

### 4.2 Physical Cards

Physical Brex cards are available for team members who travel frequently (more than once per quarter). Request through #finance on Slack.

### 4.3 Prohibited Uses

Company cards must **never** be used for:

- Personal purchases (even if you intend to reimburse)
- Cash advances or ATM withdrawals
- Cryptocurrency purchases
- Political contributions
- Gambling

Misuse of company cards will result in card revocation and disciplinary action.

## 5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are explicitly **not reimbursable**:

- First-class airfare
- Personal travel expenses attached to business trips
- Traffic or parking violations
- Personal phone plans (unless pre-approved for specific roles)
- Clothing (unless required safety gear)
- Pet care during business travel
- Spouse/partner travel expenses
- Personal entertainment
- Upgrades to premium airline lounges (use your own memberships)
- Gifts to Acme Corp team members (see Gifts and Recognition policy)
- Late fees or interest charges on personal cards used for business expenses

## 6. Tax Implications

### 6.1 Taxable Stipends

Some stipends are treated as taxable income. The following are grossed up for tax where legally permissible:

- Wellness stipend
- Internet reimbursement
- Home office setup and equipment refresh
- Childcare stipend

### 6.2 Non-Taxable Reimbursements

The following are generally non-taxable (varies by jurisdiction):

- Business travel expenses
- Client entertainment
- Professional development

### 6.3 Record Keeping

Maintain copies of all receipts and expense documentation for **7 years** for tax purposes. Expensify retains digital copies, but keeping personal backups is recommended.

## 7. Auditing

### 7.1 Regular Audits

Finance conducts monthly audits of expense reports. Approximately 20% of reports are selected for detailed review each month.

### 7.2 Red Flags

The following patterns trigger additional scrutiny:

- Expenses consistently at or just below approval thresholds
- High volume of missing receipt affidavits
- Unusual spending patterns compared to role and team norms
- Expenses submitted on the 59th or 60th day
- Round-number expenses (e.g., exactly $100.00 repeatedly)

### 7.3 Violations

| Severity | Example | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Late submission, missing receipt | Coaching conversation |
| Moderate | Exceeding limits without pre-approval, miscategorization | Written warning |
| Serious | Submitting personal expenses as business | Final warning + repayment |
| Severe | Falsifying receipts or reports | Immediate termination |

## 8. Exceptions

Legitimate exceptions to this policy exist. The process:

1. Email finance@acmecorp.com with the request and justification
2. Include your manager's written approval
3. Finance will respond within 3 business days
4. Approved exceptions are valid for the specific instance only — they do not create precedent

Common approved exceptions:
- Business-class flights for team members with documented medical needs
- Higher hotel rates when the company rate is unavailable during peak events
- Over-limit meals for large client dinners pre-approved by Sales leadership
