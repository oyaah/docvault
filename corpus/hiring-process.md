## 1. Hiring Philosophy

Hiring is the single highest-leverage activity a manager performs. A great hire compounds value for years; a bad hire drains the team for months. We invest heavily in getting it right.

### 1.1 Principles

1. **Hire for the role, not the resume.** Pedigree is a weak signal. Demonstrated ability is a strong one.
2. **Structured over unstructured.** Every interview uses a scorecard. Gut feelings are documented as data points, not used as sole decision criteria.
3. **Diversity is a strength.** Homogeneous teams are fragile. We actively work to build teams that reflect the world we serve.
4. **Speed matters.** Top candidates have multiple options. Our target is **offer within 15 business days** of the first screen.
5. **Transparency with candidates.** We tell candidates exactly what the process looks like, how long it takes, and what we're evaluating at each stage.
6. **Every interaction is a reflection of the company.** A rejected candidate should still think Acme Corp is a great company.

## 2. Headcount Planning

### 2.1 Requesting a New Role

Before a role is opened, the following must be approved:

1. **Business case:** Why is this role needed? What happens if we don't fill it?
2. **Level and compensation range:** Based on the career framework and compensation bands
3. **Location requirements:** Any time zone or geographic constraints
4. **Budget approval:** VP sign-off for backfills, SVP sign-off for net-new headcount

Headcount is planned annually during the budget cycle (October-November) for the following year. Mid-year headcount requests require VP approval and CFO review.

### 2.2 Job Description

Every job posting includes:

- **Role summary** (2-3 sentences, no buzzwords)
- **Responsibilities** (5-7 bullet points, specific and actionable)
- **Requirements** (must-have qualifications — keep this list short)
- **Nice-to-haves** (clearly labeled as optional)
- **Compensation range** (published in the posting — this is non-negotiable)
- **Benefits summary** with link to full benefits page
- **Equal opportunity statement**

Job descriptions are reviewed for inclusive language using Textio before posting. Gendered terms, unnecessary requirements (e.g., "10+ years experience" for a mid-level role), and exclusionary language are flagged and revised.

## 3. Sourcing

### 3.1 Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Use Case | Typical Yield |
|---|---|---|
| Careers page | Inbound applicants | 35% of hires |
| LinkedIn Recruiter | Outbound sourcing for senior/specialized roles | 25% of hires |
| Employee referrals | Warm introductions from the team | 20% of hires |
| Job boards (Wellfound, HN, RemoteOK) | Inbound from targeted communities | 10% of hires |
| Recruiting agencies | Hard-to-fill roles only | 5% of hires |
| Events and conferences | Brand awareness and passive candidate pipeline | 5% of hires |

### 3.2 Employee Referral Program

Team members are encouraged to refer qualified candidates. Referral bonuses:

| Role Level | Bonus |
|---|---|
| IC1-IC2 | $2,000 |
| IC3-IC4 | $4,000 |
| IC5+ | $6,000 |
| Manager (M1-M2) | $4,000 |
| Director+ (M3+) | $8,000 |

Bonuses are paid in two installments: 50% after the referred candidate's start date and 50% after 90 days of employment. Referral bonuses are taxable income.

Referrals from hiring managers for their own team are eligible for the referral bonus. Executive team members referring candidates for their own org are not eligible.

### 3.3 Diversity Sourcing

For every role, the sourcing team must:

- Ensure at least **30% of the candidate pipeline** is from underrepresented groups
- Post on diversity-focused job boards (Jopwell, Hire Autism, DiversityJobs)
- Partner with HBCUs, coding bootcamps, and community organizations
- Review sourcing outreach templates for inclusive language quarterly

If the 30% pipeline target is not met after 2 weeks of sourcing, the Talent Acquisition team and hiring manager meet to adjust the sourcing strategy.

## 4. Interview Process

### 4.1 Standard Process

The standard interview process has **4 stages** and targets completion within **15 business days** from recruiter screen to offer.

| Stage | Format | Duration | Interviewer | What We're Assessing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Recruiter Screen | Video call | 30 min | Recruiter | Role fit, motivation, compensation alignment, logistics |
| 2. Hiring Manager Screen | Video call | 45 min | Hiring manager | Technical depth, problem-solving approach, communication |
| 3. Technical / Functional Assessment | Async or live (role-dependent) | 60-90 min | 2 interviewers | Role-specific skills (see Section 4.2) |
| 4. Values & Team Fit | Video call (2 sessions) | 45 min each | 2-3 cross-functional interviewers | Collaboration, communication, values alignment |

### 4.2 Technical Assessments by Function

#### Engineering

- **Option A (preferred):** Take-home project scoped to 2-3 hours. The candidate builds something relevant to the role (e.g., a small API, a data pipeline, a frontend component). Followed by a 60-minute live code review and extension discussion.
- **Option B:** Live pair programming session (90 minutes) working on a real problem from our codebase (sanitized). No algorithmic puzzles or whiteboard coding.
- **What we're looking for:** Code quality, system design thinking, testing approach, communication of tradeoffs, ability to ask good questions.
- **What we're NOT looking for:** Memorized algorithms, specific framework experience (can be learned), speed over quality.

#### Design

- Portfolio review (60 minutes): Walk through 2-3 case studies, focusing on process, decisions, and outcomes — not just the visual output.
- Design exercise (async, 3-4 hours): A realistic design challenge relevant to our product. Present findings in a 45-minute follow-up.

#### Sales

- Mock discovery call (30 minutes): Candidate runs a discovery call with a team member playing the prospect.
- Mock demo (30 minutes): Candidate demos a product they know well (not necessarily ours) to assess presentation and storytelling ability.
- Pipeline review (30 minutes): Candidate walks through how they would build and manage a pipeline for a given territory.

#### Marketing

- Content or campaign analysis (async, 2-3 hours): Analyze a real campaign (ours or a competitor's) and present findings and recommendations.
- Strategy presentation (45 minutes): Present a go-to-market strategy for a hypothetical product launch.

#### People Operations / Finance / Legal

- Case study (async, 2-3 hours): A scenario-based exercise relevant to the role.
- Functional interview (60 minutes): Deep dive into functional expertise with 2 interviewers.

### 4.3 Interviewer Training

All interviewers must complete the following before conducting interviews:

1. **Interview Fundamentals** training (2-hour workshop, live or recorded)
2. **Unconscious Bias in Hiring** training (1-hour module)
3. **Shadow 2 interviews** with a trained interviewer
4. **Be shadowed for 1 interview** by a trained interviewer who provides feedback

Interviewers who have not completed training are not permitted to participate in the interview process. Interviewer training is refreshed annually.

### 4.4 Scorecards

Every interviewer completes a scorecard within **24 hours** of the interview. Scorecards include:

- Rating on each assessed dimension (Strong Yes / Yes / Mixed / No / Strong No)
- Specific evidence supporting each rating
- Overall recommendation (Hire / No Hire / Need More Info)
- Notes on any concerns or open questions

Scorecards are submitted **before** the debrief meeting to prevent anchoring bias.

### 4.5 Debrief and Decision

Within 2 business days of the final interview:

1. All interviewers submit scorecards (if not already done — this is a hard requirement)
2. The recruiter compiles a candidate summary with all scorecard data
3. A 30-minute debrief meeting is held with all interviewers and the hiring manager
4. Each interviewer shares their independent assessment
5. The hiring manager makes the final decision

**Decision framework:**

- **Unanimous Strong Yes or Yes:** Proceed to offer
- **Mixed with majority positive:** Hiring manager decides; must document the reasoning for proceeding despite concerns
- **Any Strong No:** The Strong No interviewer explains their reasoning. If the concern is valid and specific, it is typically a rejection. "I just didn't feel it" is not a Strong No.
- **Unanimous No:** Reject

The hiring manager owns the final call. The debrief is advisory, but decisions that override a majority of the panel must be documented and reviewed by the Talent Acquisition lead.

## 5. Candidate Experience

### 5.1 Communication Standards

| Touchpoint | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Application acknowledgment | Within 24 hours (automated) |
| Initial screen scheduling | Within 5 business days of application |
| Post-screen decision | Within 3 business days |
| Interview scheduling | Within 5 business days of advancing |
| Post-final-round decision | Within 5 business days |
| Offer extended | Within 2 business days of decision |
| Rejection notification | Within 2 business days of decision |

### 5.2 Rejection Communication

Every rejected candidate receives:

- A personalized email (not a template) from the recruiter
- Specific, constructive feedback if requested (for candidates who completed at least Stage 2)
- An invitation to apply for future roles

We do not ghost candidates. If a process is delayed, proactively communicate the delay and new timeline.

### 5.3 Candidate Feedback

After the process concludes (whether hired or not), candidates receive a brief survey:

- How would you rate your overall interview experience? (1-5)
- Was the process clearly communicated?
- Did you feel the interviewers were prepared?
- Would you recommend Acme Corp as a place to interview?

Results are reviewed monthly by the Talent Acquisition team. Our target is a **4.2+ average** across all respondents. Current average (2025 Q3): **4.4**.

## 6. Offers

### 6.1 Compensation Determination

Offers are based on:

1. **Role and level** as defined in the career framework
2. **Location factor** per the compensation calculator
3. **Experience and scope** — where within the band the candidate falls
4. **Internal equity** — what peers at the same level and location earn
5. **Market data** — Radford, Pave, and Levels.fyi benchmarks (targeting 65th percentile for base, 75th for total compensation)

### 6.2 Offer Components

| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Base salary | Per compensation calculator |
| Equity | Stock options, 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff |
| Sign-on bonus | Case-by-case for senior hires (typically $5,000-$25,000) |
| Benefits | Full benefits effective day 1 |
| Start date | Flexible, typically 2-4 weeks from acceptance |

### 6.3 Offer Approval

| Level | Approver |
|---|---|
| IC1-IC3 | Hiring manager + Director |
| IC4-IC5 | Director + VP |
| IC6+ | VP + CEO |
| M1-M2 | Director + VP |
| M3+ | VP + CEO |

### 6.4 Negotiation

We publish compensation ranges in job postings and do not negotiate above the posted range. Within the range, we position based on experience and internal equity. We explain our compensation philosophy transparently during the offer conversation.

We do not ask candidates about their current compensation. In jurisdictions where salary history inquiries are restricted by law, we comply fully. In all other jurisdictions, we follow the same practice voluntarily.

### 6.5 Offer Expiration

Offers are valid for **7 calendar days** from the date of the offer letter. Extensions of up to 7 additional days may be granted by the recruiter upon request.

## 7. Onboarding

### 7.1 Pre-boarding (Before Day 1)

After offer acceptance, the onboarding team initiates:

- Equipment order and shipping (laptop, YubiKey)
- Account provisioning (Google Workspace, Slack, Okta)
- Welcome email with Day 1 logistics
- Onboarding buddy assignment (a peer on the team, not the manager)
- First-week calendar with scheduled meetings and orientations

### 7.2 Week 1

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Welcome session (People Ops), security onboarding, tool setup |
| Tuesday | Team introduction, meet your manager, onboarding buddy lunch |
| Wednesday | Product overview, codebase walkthrough (Engineering) or function-specific orientation |
| Thursday | First 1:1 with manager, set 30/60/90 day goals |
| Friday | No meetings — explore the handbook, read documentation, get settled |

### 7.3 First 90 Days

**30 days:** Learn the landscape. Understand the product, the team, the codebase/function, and the key stakeholders. Ship at least one small contribution (code change, document, campaign asset — role-dependent).

**60 days:** Contribute independently. Own a defined piece of work. Attend or lead a team meeting. Identify one process improvement.

**90 days:** Operate at full speed. Complete the probationary review with your manager. Set OKRs for the next quarter.

### 7.4 Probationary Period

All new hires have a **6-month probationary period**. During this time:

- Formal check-ins with the manager at 30, 60, 90, and 180 days
- Either party may end the employment relationship with 2 weeks notice
- At the end of 6 months, the manager confirms successful completion or initiates separation

The probationary period is not a trial by fire — it is a structured ramp-up. The goal is for the new hire to succeed, and the manager is responsible for providing the support to make that happen.

## 8. Rehiring

Former team members in good standing are eligible for rehire. Benefits of rehiring:

- Abbreviated interview process (hiring manager screen + values interview only)
- Previous tenure counts toward sabbatical eligibility (if rehired within 2 years)
- Skip onboarding components already completed

Former team members who were terminated for cause or who resigned during a PIP are not eligible for rehire.

## 10. Legal and Compliance

### 10.1 Equal Employment Opportunity

Acme Corp is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, veteran status, age, or any other legally protected characteristic.

### 10.2 Accommodation

We provide reasonable accommodations for candidates with disabilities throughout the interview process. Candidates may request accommodations at any stage by contacting their recruiter or accommodations@acmecorp.com.

Examples of accommodations we've provided:

- Extended time for take-home assessments
- Sign language interpreters for video interviews
- Written materials in alternative formats
- Modified interview schedules

### 10.3 Background Checks

Offers are contingent on the successful completion of a background check. Checks include:

- Identity verification
- Criminal history (where legally permissible and role-relevant)
- Employment verification (last 2 positions)
- Education verification (highest degree claimed)

Background checks are conducted by Checkr and typically complete within 5 business days. A finding on a background check does not automatically disqualify a candidate — results are reviewed individually considering the nature of the finding, its relevance to the role, and the time elapsed.

### 10.4 Immigration

Acme Corp sponsors work visas for candidates who require authorization to work in the country where the role is based. Visa sponsorship is evaluated on a case-by-case basis considering role criticality and processing timelines. We work with Fragomen for all immigration matters.
