## 1. Security Philosophy

Security is everyone's job, not just the Security team's. As an all-remote company handling sensitive customer data, our attack surface is distributed across hundreds of home offices in dozens of countries. This makes security hygiene at the individual level critical.

We follow a **zero-trust** model: no network, device, or user is inherently trusted. Every access request is verified regardless of origin.

### 1.1 Security Principles

1. **Least privilege:** Grant only the minimum access needed to perform a role
2. **Defense in depth:** No single control is the last line of defense
3. **Assume breach:** Design systems as if an attacker is already inside
4. **Transparency:** Security incidents are disclosed internally within 24 hours and externally per legal and contractual obligations
5. **Blameless culture:** Reporting a security concern is always the right call. We never punish someone for surfacing a vulnerability or mistake.

## 2. Access Management

### 2.1 Identity and Authentication

All team members must use the following authentication standards:

| System | Requirement |
|---|---|
| SSO (Okta) | Primary identity provider — all SaaS tools go through Okta |
| Multi-factor authentication | Hardware key (YubiKey) required for Okta, GitHub, AWS, and GCP |
| Passwords | Minimum 16 characters, generated by 1Password. No reuse. |
| SSH keys | Ed25519 keys with passphrases, rotated annually |
| API tokens | Scoped to minimum permissions, 90-day maximum expiry |

**Password manager:** All team members must use **1Password Teams** for credential storage. Credentials stored in browsers, text files, or Slack messages are a policy violation.

### 2.2 Access Provisioning

Access to systems is provisioned based on role:

1. **Day 1 access:** Email, Slack, Google Workspace, 1Password, BambooHR, the handbook
2. **Role-based access:** Requested by the manager through the IT Access Request form in Jira
3. **Elevated access:** Production systems, customer data, admin consoles — requires Security team approval
4. **Temporary access:** Time-boxed access for specific projects, auto-revoked after the specified period

All access requests are logged and auditable.

### 2.3 Access Reviews

| Review Type | Frequency | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Manager access review | Quarterly | All direct reports' access across systems |
| Privileged access review | Monthly | Production, admin, and root-level access |
| Vendor access review | Semi-annual | Third-party system integrations |
| Dormant account review | Monthly | Accounts with no login in 60+ days |

Accounts with no activity for 90 days are automatically suspended. Accounts not reactivated within 30 days of suspension are permanently deprovisioned.

### 2.4 Offboarding

When a team member leaves the company:

- All access is revoked within **1 hour** of the official separation time
- Company devices are remotely wiped
- SSH keys and API tokens are revoked
- Shared credentials in 1Password vaults accessible to the departing team member are rotated within 24 hours
- A deprovisioning checklist is completed by IT and reviewed by Security

## 3. Device Security

### 3.1 Company Laptops

All company-issued laptops must have:

| Control | Requirement |
|---|---|
| OS updates | Applied within 7 days of release; critical security patches within 48 hours |
| Full-disk encryption | FileVault (macOS) or BitLocker (Windows) enabled — verified by MDM |
| Screen lock | Maximum 5-minute idle timeout |
| Firewall | Enabled at all times |
| Antivirus/EDR | CrowdStrike Falcon installed and running |
| MDM | Mosyle (macOS) or Intune (Windows) enrolled |
| VPN | Tailscale installed for accessing internal services |

### 3.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)

Personal devices used for company work must meet the standards outlined in the Remote Work Policy, Section 6.2. Additionally:

- No company data may be stored locally on personal devices (use web-based access only)
- Personal devices must be enrolled in MDM for remote wipe capability
- Jailbroken or rooted devices are prohibited

### 3.3 Mobile Devices

Company email and Slack on mobile devices require:

- Screen lock with biometric or 6+ digit PIN
- Remote wipe capability (MDM enrollment)
- No sideloaded apps
- OS version no more than 1 major version behind current

### 3.4 Lost or Stolen Devices

Report lost or stolen devices **immediately** (within 1 hour of discovery):

1. Contact IT Security: #security-incidents on Slack or security-emergency@acmecorp.com
2. IT will initiate a remote wipe
3. File a police report if theft is suspected
4. A replacement device will be shipped within 2 business days

## 4. Data Classification

### 4.1 Classification Levels

| Level | Description | Examples | Handling |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Information intended for public consumption | Marketing materials, blog posts, open-source code | No restrictions |
| **Internal** | Information for Acme Corp team members only | Handbook, internal presentations, OKRs | No sharing outside company |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business information | Financial data, strategy documents, HR records | Need-to-know access, encrypted storage |
| **Restricted** | Highest sensitivity | Customer PII, credentials, security audit results, encryption keys | Strict access control, encrypted in transit and at rest, audit logging |

### 4.2 Data Handling Requirements

| Action | Internal | Confidential | Restricted |
|---|---|---|---|
| Storage | Company-approved systems | Encrypted at rest | Encrypted, access-logged |
| Sharing internally | Slack, Google Drive | Google Drive with restricted sharing | Dedicated secure channels only |
| Sharing externally | With manager approval | With Legal approval | With CISO approval |
| Printing | Permitted | Discouraged | Prohibited |
| Retention | Per retention schedule | Per retention schedule | Minimum necessary, destroyed after use |

### 4.3 Customer Data

Customer data is classified as **Restricted** by default. Specific rules:

- Never copy customer data to local devices
- Never share customer data in Slack (even in private channels)
- Access customer data only when required for your role and for a specific task
- All access to customer data is logged and auditable
- Customer data must not leave approved production systems except through approved export processes
- Test environments must use synthetic data, never production customer data

## 5. Network Security

### 5.1 VPN

All access to internal services requires a VPN connection through Tailscale. Tailscale provides:

- WireGuard-based encrypted tunnels
- Zero-trust network access (device identity verified, not just network location)
- Automatic key rotation
- Split tunneling for personal traffic (company traffic only goes through the tunnel)

### 5.2 Public Wi-Fi

Working from public Wi-Fi (coffee shops, airports, hotels) is permitted **only** with the VPN active. Without a VPN:

- Do not access internal tools or services
- Do not log in to any company account
- Do not access customer data

### 5.3 Home Network

Recommended (not required) home network security measures:

- Router firmware updated to latest version
- WPA3 or WPA2-AES encryption (not WEP or WPA-TKIP)
- Default router admin credentials changed
- IoT devices on a separate network segment
- DNS filtering enabled (e.g., NextDNS, Pi-hole)

## 6. Application Security

### 6.1 Secure Development Lifecycle

All code changes must follow:

1. **Peer review:** Every merge request requires at least one approval from a reviewer who is not the author
2. **Static analysis:** Semgrep runs on every merge request; no high-severity findings may be merged
3. **Dependency scanning:** Dependabot alerts must be addressed within SLA (Critical: 48 hours, High: 7 days, Medium: 30 days, Low: 90 days)
4. **Secret scanning:** GitLeaks runs pre-commit; any detected secret blocks the commit
5. **Container scanning:** Trivy scans all container images before deployment

### 6.2 Production Access

- No direct SSH access to production servers (use bastion hosts with session recording)
- Database access through read-only replicas for debugging; write access requires a runbook and approval
- All production changes go through CI/CD — no manual deployments
- Infrastructure as Code (Terraform) for all cloud resource provisioning

### 6.3 Secrets Management

- Application secrets stored in **HashiCorp Vault**
- Secrets injected at runtime, never baked into images or committed to repos
- Secret rotation schedule: 90 days for application secrets, 30 days for privileged credentials
- Emergency rotation capability: all production secrets can be rotated within 4 hours

## 7. Incident Response

### 7.1 Reporting an Incident

Anyone who suspects a security incident must report it immediately:

- **Slack:** #security-incidents (24/7 monitored)
- **Email:** security@acmecorp.com
- **Phone:** +1-555-SEC-RITY (for critical incidents outside business hours)
- **Anonymous:** ethics@acmecorp.com

### 7.2 Incident Severity Levels

| Severity | Description | Response Time | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 - Critical | Active breach, data exfiltration, service compromise | 15 minutes | Unauthorized access to customer database |
| P2 - High | Potential breach, vulnerable system exposed | 1 hour | Credentials leaked in public repository |
| P3 - Medium | Security control failure, policy violation | 4 hours | Laptop lost without confirmed data exposure |
| P4 - Low | Minor policy violation, informational | 24 hours | Team member clicked a phishing simulation link |

### 7.3 Incident Response Process

1. **Detection:** Automated alerting or human report
2. **Triage:** Security on-call assesses severity and impact (within response time SLA)
3. **Containment:** Isolate affected systems, revoke compromised credentials
4. **Investigation:** Determine root cause and scope of impact
5. **Remediation:** Fix the vulnerability, restore affected systems
6. **Communication:** Notify affected parties per legal and contractual obligations
7. **Post-incident review:** Blameless retrospective within 5 business days
8. **Documentation:** Full incident report published internally

### 7.4 Breach Notification

| Audience | Timeline | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Internal (all-hands) | 24 hours | CISO |
| Affected customers | 72 hours (per GDPR) or as required by law | Legal + CISO |
| Regulatory bodies | Per jurisdictional requirements | Legal |
| Public disclosure | Case-by-case, typically within 30 days | Comms + Legal |

## 8. Security Training

### 8.1 Required Training

| Training | Frequency | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| Security Awareness Fundamentals | Annual | All team members |
| Phishing Recognition | Quarterly (simulated phishing + debrief) | All team members |
| Secure Coding Practices | Annual | Engineering |
| Incident Response Tabletop | Semi-annual | Engineering + Security |
| Data Handling and Privacy | Annual | All team members |
| Social Engineering Defense | Annual | Customer-facing roles |

### 8.2 New Hire Security Onboarding

Within the first week, all new hires must complete:

1. Security Awareness Fundamentals course (1 hour)
2. 1Password setup and credential migration
3. YubiKey enrollment
4. MDM enrollment
5. VPN (Tailscale) setup
6. Acknowledge the Acceptable Use Policy

### 8.3 Phishing Simulations

The Security team runs monthly phishing simulations. Results are aggregated anonymously. Team members who click on simulated phishing links receive additional training — there is no punitive action, but repeated failures (3+ in a 12-month period) trigger a mandatory 1:1 coaching session with Security.

## 9. Compliance

### 9.1 Frameworks

Acme Corp maintains compliance with:

- **SOC 2 Type II** — Annual audit, most recent report: October 2025
- **ISO 27001** — Certified since 2023, next recertification: March 2026
- **GDPR** — EU data protection regulation
- **CCPA/CPRA** — California consumer privacy
- **HIPAA** — For customers in healthcare (BAA available)

### 9.2 Vendor Security Reviews

Before onboarding any new vendor that will access company or customer data:

1. Complete the Vendor Security Questionnaire
2. Review the vendor's SOC 2 report or equivalent
3. Security team risk assessment
4. Legal review of DPA (Data Processing Agreement)
5. Approval by CISO for Restricted-level data access

### 9.3 Penetration Testing

- **External penetration testing:** Annually by an independent firm (most recent: August 2025 by NCC Group)
- **Bug bounty program:** Ongoing through HackerOne (public program, $500-$15,000 bounties)
- **Internal red team exercises:** Quarterly

## 10. Acceptable Use

### 10.1 Permitted Use

Company systems and accounts may be used for:

- Work-related activities
- Reasonable personal use that does not interfere with work or violate any policy
- Professional development and learning

### 10.2 Prohibited Use

Company systems and accounts must never be used for:

- Illegal activities
- Harassment or discrimination
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Torrenting or pirating content
- Storing personal files in company cloud storage (beyond incidental)
- Running personal businesses or side projects on company infrastructure
