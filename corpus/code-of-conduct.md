# Code of Conduct

**Policy Owner:** Legal & People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2025-06-01  
**Effective Date:** 2021-01-01  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Applies To:** All team members, contractors, and anyone representing Acme Corp  

---

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct defines the behavioral standards expected of every person who works at or represents Acme Corp. It is not a comprehensive list of rules — it is a framework for making good decisions when the right course of action isn't obvious.

The simplest test: **Would you be comfortable if your actions were visible to the entire company?**

## 2. Core Standards

### 2.1 Respect and Professionalism

- Treat every person with dignity regardless of role, level, tenure, or background
- Disagree with ideas, not with people. "That approach has a flaw" not "You don't understand the problem"
- Harassment, discrimination, bullying, and retaliation are zero-tolerance violations
- This applies everywhere: Slack, video calls, offsites, social events, and external interactions

### 2.2 Harassment Policy

Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

Examples of prohibited conduct:

- Sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or sexually suggestive comments
- Slurs, epithets, or derogatory remarks related to any protected characteristic
- Displaying offensive images, symbols, or materials (including in virtual backgrounds, Slack profiles, or avatars)
- Intimidation, threats, or hostile behavior
- Unwelcome physical contact (at in-person events)
- Deliberate misgendering or deadnaming after being corrected
- Microaggressions that create a pattern of exclusion

One incident can constitute harassment if it is sufficiently severe. A pattern of less severe behavior also constitutes harassment.

### 2.3 Discrimination

All employment decisions — hiring, promotion, compensation, termination, and work assignments — are based on merit, qualifications, and business needs. Discrimination based on any protected characteristic is prohibited.

This includes both:

- **Direct discrimination:** Treating someone differently because of a protected characteristic
- **Indirect discrimination:** Applying a seemingly neutral policy that disproportionately impacts a protected group without business justification

### 2.4 Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern, participates in an investigation, or exercises their rights under this policy is strictly prohibited. Retaliation includes:

- Adverse changes to work assignments, schedule, or responsibilities
- Exclusion from meetings, projects, or social activities
- Negative performance evaluations that are not supported by evidence
- Hostile behavior or silent treatment

Retaliation is treated as seriously as the underlying conduct being reported.

## 3. Conflicts of Interest

### 3.1 Definition

A conflict of interest exists when your personal interests — financial, familial, or otherwise — could influence or appear to influence your professional judgment.

### 3.2 Common Scenarios

| Scenario | Action Required |
|---|---|
| You have a financial interest in a vendor being evaluated | Disclose to your manager and recuse yourself from the decision |
| A family member applies for a role at Acme Corp | Disclose to Talent Acquisition; you may not be involved in the hiring process |
| You're offered a board seat at another company | Obtain written approval from your VP and Legal |
| You're starting a side project in a related space | Disclose to your manager and Legal for conflict review |
| You receive a gift from a vendor worth more than $100 | Decline or return it; disclose to your manager |

### 3.3 Outside Employment and Side Projects

Side projects and outside employment are permitted as long as they:

- Do not compete with Acme Corp's business
- Do not use Acme Corp resources (time, equipment, IP, confidential information)
- Do not interfere with your job performance
- Are disclosed to your manager

Open-source contributions are encouraged and do not require disclosure unless they involve a competing project.

## 4. Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Confidential Information

You have a duty to protect confidential information, both during and after your employment. Confidential information includes:

- Product roadmaps and unreleased features
- Financial data (revenue, margins, projections)
- Customer data and contracts
- Employee compensation and personal information
- Strategic plans and M&A discussions
- Security vulnerabilities (before remediation)
- Board materials and investor communications

### 4.2 Intellectual Property

All work product created during your employment using company resources or related to company business is the property of Acme Corp. This is governed by the Proprietary Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (PIIA) signed at hire.

Pre-existing IP and personal projects created on your own time with your own resources are yours, provided they don't conflict with Section 3.3.

### 4.3 Insider Trading

If you have access to material non-public information (MNPI) about Acme Corp or any other company, you may not trade securities based on that information. This applies to our stock (when publicly traded) and to the stock of partners, customers, or acquisition targets.

A quarterly trading blackout period is enforced for team members with access to financial data. The CFO's office publishes the blackout calendar.

## 5. Reporting Concerns

### 5.1 How to Report

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code, you have multiple reporting channels:

| Channel | Best For | Anonymous? |
|---|---|---|
| Your manager | Day-to-day concerns, minor issues | No |
| Skip-level manager | Concerns involving your manager | No |
| People Operations (people-ops@acmecorp.com) | HR-related issues, harassment, discrimination | No |
| Legal (legal@acmecorp.com) | Compliance concerns, conflicts of interest | No |
| Ethics hotline (ethics@acmecorp.com) | Any concern, especially when other channels feel unsafe | Yes |
| External hotline (1-800-555-ETHX) | Operated by a third party; for when you don't trust internal channels | Yes |

### 5.2 What Happens After a Report

1. **Acknowledgment:** Within 24 hours (48 hours for anonymous reports via hotline)
2. **Assessment:** People Operations and/or Legal assess the report and determine investigation scope
3. **Investigation:** Conducted by a trained investigator (internal or external depending on severity). Typical timeline: 2-4 weeks.
4. **Confidentiality:** Investigations are conducted confidentially. Information is shared only with those who need to know.
5. **Resolution:** Findings are documented. If a violation occurred, appropriate action is taken (see Section 5.3).
6. **Follow-up:** The reporter is informed of the outcome to the extent possible while respecting privacy.

### 5.3 Disciplinary Actions

| Severity | Examples | Possible Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Unprofessional language, minor policy breach | Coaching, verbal warning |
| Moderate | Repeated minor violations, conflicts of interest not disclosed | Written warning, mandatory training |
| Serious | Harassment, discrimination, data breach, fraud | Suspension, demotion, termination |
| Severe | Criminal conduct, sexual harassment, retaliation | Immediate termination, legal referral |

## 6. Workplace Safety

### 6.1 At In-Person Events

During company offsites, summits, and team gatherings:

- Alcohol consumption is permitted but moderation is expected. Intoxication is not acceptable.
- All activities must be optional. No one should feel pressured to participate in physical activities, drinking, or social events.
- Consent matters in all interactions.
- The Code of Conduct applies in full — work events are work, even if they feel social.

### 6.2 Psychological Safety

We are committed to creating an environment where every team member feels safe to:

- Ask questions without fear of being judged
- Admit mistakes without fear of punishment
- Offer dissenting opinions without fear of retaliation
- Be themselves without fear of exclusion

Managers are specifically responsible for fostering psychological safety on their teams. This is evaluated in 360 feedback and engagement surveys.

## 7. Environmental and Social Responsibility

### 7.1 Environmental Commitment

As an all-remote company, our direct environmental footprint is relatively small. We commit to:

- Carbon offsetting for all company travel (flights, ground transportation)
- Sustainable procurement preferences when selecting vendors
- E-waste recycling for all returned company equipment
- Annual sustainability report (published in the handbook)

### 7.2 Community Engagement

- 2 paid volunteer days per year per team member
- Charitable donation matching up to $1,000/year
- Pro-bono program for nonprofits (team members may dedicate up to 5% of work time to approved pro-bono projects)

## 8. Compliance with Laws

### 8.1 Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

Acme Corp complies with the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act, and all applicable anti-corruption laws. Specifically:

- Never offer, promise, or give anything of value to a government official to influence a decision
- Never accept bribes or kickbacks
- Facilitation payments are prohibited
- Third-party agents and consultants must agree to our anti-corruption terms

### 8.2 Trade Compliance

We comply with all applicable trade sanctions, export controls, and import regulations. If you're unsure whether a transaction, customer, or partner is subject to restrictions, contact Legal before proceeding.

### 8.3 Data Privacy

We comply with GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and all applicable data privacy laws. See the Security Practices document for detailed data handling requirements.

## 9. Annual Acknowledgment

Every team member must acknowledge this Code of Conduct annually. The acknowledgment confirms that you:

- Have read and understand the Code
- Will comply with its provisions
- Will report any known or suspected violations
- Understand that violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination

The annual acknowledgment is distributed in January via BambooHR. Completion is tracked and required for all team members.
