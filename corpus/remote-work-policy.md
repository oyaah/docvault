# Remote Work Policy

**Policy Owner:** People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-15  
**Effective Date:** 2021-01-01  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Applies To:** All team members globally  

---

## 1. Overview

Acme Corp operates as an all-remote company with no physical offices. Every team member works from a location of their choosing — whether that is a home office, a co-working space, or a coffee shop. This policy outlines the expectations, guidelines, and support structures that make all-remote work sustainable at scale.

We believe that talent is evenly distributed but opportunity is not. All-remote work allows us to hire the best people regardless of geography, and to give every team member autonomy over their environment and schedule.

## 2. Guiding Principles

- **Async-first:** Default to asynchronous communication. Meetings are the last resort, not the first.
- **Written over verbal:** Decisions, context, and outcomes must be documented in writing.
- **Results over hours:** We measure output, not time spent at a desk.
- **Handbook-first:** If a process isn't in the handbook, it doesn't exist. Document it or it didn't happen.
- **Low-context communication:** Write as if the reader has no prior context. Over-communicate intent.
- **Transparency by default:** Information is public within the company unless there is a specific reason to restrict it.

## 3. Eligibility

All team members — full-time employees, part-time employees, and long-term contractors — are eligible for remote work. There are no roles that require physical presence at a company location because there are no company locations.

### 3.1 Contractor Considerations

Independent contractors must comply with their local labor laws regarding workspace safety and insurance. Acme Corp does not provide equipment or stipends to contractors unless specified in the contract.

## 4. Workspace Requirements

### 4.1 Minimum Requirements

Every team member must maintain a workspace that meets the following criteria:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Internet speed | Minimum 50 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload |
| Backup connectivity | A secondary internet option (mobile hotspot, nearby co-working space) |
| Audio quality | A dedicated headset or microphone for calls |
| Video capability | A webcam capable of 720p or higher |
| Privacy | A space where confidential conversations can be held without being overheard |
| Ergonomics | A desk and chair suitable for full-day work |
| Power | Reliable electricity with a plan for outages (UPS recommended) |

### 4.2 Home Office Setup

We provide a one-time home office stipend of $1,500 USD (or local equivalent) for new hires to set up their workspace. This can be used for:

- Desk and ergonomic chair
- Monitor(s) and monitor arms
- Keyboard and mouse
- Lighting
- Webcam and headset
- Cable management and accessories

After the initial stipend, team members receive an annual equipment refresh allowance of $500 USD. Unused portions do not roll over.

### 4.3 Co-working Spaces

Team members who prefer to work from a co-working space may expense up to $300 USD per month. This requires:

1. Pre-approval from your manager (one-time, not monthly)
2. Submission of receipts through the expense system
3. The co-working space must meet the workspace requirements above

### 4.4 Internet Reimbursement

We reimburse internet costs up to $75 USD per month. Submit your monthly internet bill through the expense system. If your internet plan is shared with a household, we reimburse 50% of the total cost up to the cap.

## 5. Working Hours and Availability

### 5.1 Core Collaboration Window

There is no mandatory company-wide working hours requirement. However, each team has a **core collaboration window** — a 4-hour block where synchronous availability is expected. This window is set by the team lead in consultation with the team and documented in the team's handbook page.

Examples of core collaboration windows:

- **Engineering Platform:** 14:00–18:00 UTC
- **Customer Success EMEA:** 09:00–13:00 UTC
- **Marketing:** 15:00–19:00 UTC

### 5.2 Communicating Availability

Team members must:

- Keep their Slack status updated to reflect their current availability
- Block personal time on their calendar so colleagues know when not to schedule meetings
- Set working hours in Google Calendar so meeting invitations show conflicts
- Respond to Slack messages within **4 business hours** during their working day
- Respond to email within **24 business hours**

### 5.3 Flexible Scheduling

Outside the core collaboration window, team members may structure their day however they wish. Common patterns include:

- **Split shift:** Work 4 hours in the morning, take a long break, work 4 hours in the evening
- **Compressed week:** Work longer days Monday through Thursday, take Friday off (requires manager approval)
- **Early bird / night owl:** Shift hours earlier or later to match personal productivity peaks

### 5.4 Time Zone Considerations

We do not penalize team members for being in a particular time zone. If a meeting must happen outside someone's normal working hours, the inconvenience should rotate among participants rather than always falling on the same person.

When scheduling across time zones:

- Never schedule meetings before 08:00 or after 20:00 in any participant's local time
- Record all meetings for those who cannot attend synchronously
- Post meeting notes and action items in the relevant Slack channel within 2 hours

## 6. Equipment and Technology

### 6.1 Company-Provided Equipment

All full-time team members receive:

- A laptop (MacBook Pro 14" M3 Pro or equivalent ThinkPad, based on preference)
- A YubiKey for hardware-based authentication
- Access to 1Password Teams for credential management

Laptops remain company property and must be returned upon separation. The equipment team ships directly to your home address. Expected delivery is within 10 business days of your start date.

### 6.2 BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)

Team members may use personal devices for non-sensitive work (Slack, email, calendar) provided:

- The device runs a supported OS version (no more than 2 major versions behind current)
- Full-disk encryption is enabled
- A screen lock is set with a maximum timeout of 5 minutes
- The device is enrolled in our MDM solution for remote wipe capability

### 6.3 Software and Subscriptions

Standard software provided to all team members:

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Slack | Asynchronous and real-time messaging |
| Google Workspace | Email, calendar, docs, sheets |
| GitLab | Source code, issue tracking, CI/CD |
| Figma | Design collaboration |
| Notion | Internal documentation and project management |
| Zoom | Video conferencing |
| 1Password | Credential management |
| Expensify | Expense reporting |

Requests for additional software must go through the IT procurement process. Tools costing under $20/month per seat can be approved by your manager. Tools above that threshold require VP-level approval.

## 7. Communication Norms

### 7.1 Default to Public Channels

Use public Slack channels for all work-related communication. Direct messages should be reserved for:

- Sensitive personal matters
- 1:1 feedback
- Quick logistical questions that are genuinely not relevant to anyone else

If a DM conversation produces a decision or useful context, summarize it in the relevant public channel.

### 7.2 Meeting Hygiene

- Every meeting must have an agenda shared at least 24 hours in advance
- No agenda, no meeting — any participant can decline
- Meetings default to 25 minutes (not 30) or 50 minutes (not 60)
- The meeting organizer is responsible for posting notes and action items afterward
- "This could have been a Slack thread" is valid feedback. Receive it gracefully.

### 7.3 Documentation Expectations

Every project, process, and decision should be documented in the handbook or the relevant Notion workspace. The standard for documentation is:

> A new team member joining tomorrow should be able to understand what was decided, why it was decided, and how to act on it — without asking anyone.

## 8. Travel and In-Person Gatherings

### 8.1 Team Offsites

Each team conducts one in-person offsite per year, typically 3-5 days. The company covers:

- Flights (economy class for flights under 6 hours, premium economy for 6+ hours)
- Hotel accommodations (up to $250/night)
- Per diem of $75/day for meals
- Ground transportation

### 8.2 Company Summit

Once per year, the entire company gathers for a 4-day summit. Location rotates annually. The 2026 summit is planned for Lisbon, Portugal (March 16-20, 2026).

### 8.3 Co-located Work Sessions

Team members who live near each other are encouraged to meet for co-working sessions. The company reimburses reasonable costs (coffee, lunch, co-working day pass) up to $50/person/session, limited to twice per month.

## 9. Relocation

### 9.1 Domestic Relocation

Team members may relocate within their country of employment at any time. Notify People Operations at least 30 days in advance so we can:

- Update tax withholding if applicable
- Adjust any location-based compensation bands
- Update emergency contact and shipping information

### 9.2 International Relocation

Moving to a different country requires approval from People Operations and Legal. Key considerations:

- We can only employ people in countries where we have a legal entity or an Employer of Record (EOR) arrangement
- Compensation may be adjusted to reflect the new location's cost-of-labor band
- Tax, benefits, and employment law implications must be reviewed
- Minimum 90 days notice required
- Not all relocations can be approved

### 9.3 Temporary Relocation

Working from a different country for up to 30 consecutive days does not require approval but does require notification. For stays of 31-90 days, manager approval is required. Stays over 90 days are treated as permanent relocation.

## 10. Compensation and Location

We use a **location-factor** model for compensation. Base pay is determined by role and level, then adjusted by a location factor that reflects the cost of labor in your geographic area. Location factors are published in the compensation calculator and updated annually.

Location factors are based on metro areas, not countries. For example:

| Location | Factor |
|---|---|
| San Francisco, CA | 1.00 |
| New York, NY | 0.97 |
| Austin, TX | 0.90 |
| London, UK | 0.95 |
| Berlin, Germany | 0.85 |
| Bangalore, India | 0.55 |
| Lagos, Nigeria | 0.50 |
| Buenos Aires, Argentina | 0.60 |

These factors apply to base salary only. Equity grants, bonuses, and stipends are not location-adjusted.

## 11. Health and Well-being

### 11.1 Ergonomic Assessments

All team members are entitled to a virtual ergonomic assessment with a certified ergonomist within their first 90 days. Follow-up assessments are available annually.

### 11.2 Mental Health Support

Remote work can be isolating. We provide:

- Access to Modern Health (6 therapy sessions and unlimited coaching per year)
- A monthly wellness stipend of $100 for gym memberships, fitness classes, meditation apps, or similar
- Slack channels for social connection (#pets, #cooking, #fitness, #parents, #gaming)
- No-meeting Fridays to protect deep work time

### 11.3 Burnout Prevention

Managers are trained to watch for signs of burnout in remote teams. Team members are expected to:

- Take their full PTO allocation (see PTO Policy)
- Disconnect fully during time off — no Slack, no email
- Set clear start and end times for their workday
- Use the "Do Not Disturb" schedule in Slack

## 12. Compliance and Legal

### 12.1 Data Protection

Team members must ensure their workspace complies with data protection requirements:

- Do not work on company data using public Wi-Fi without a VPN
- Lock your screen when stepping away, even at home
- Do not allow household members to access company devices
- Shred or securely delete any printed company documents

### 12.2 Tax Obligations

Team members are responsible for understanding their local tax obligations related to remote work. The company provides access to a tax advisor for questions about home office deductions and cross-border tax implications.

### 12.3 Employment Law

Remote work arrangements comply with the employment laws of the jurisdiction where the team member is located. In cases of conflict between this policy and local law, local law prevails.

## 13. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy are handled through the standard progressive discipline process:

1. Verbal warning with documented coaching conversation
2. Written warning with a performance improvement plan
3. Final written warning
4. Termination

Serious violations (e.g., data protection breaches, security policy violations) may result in immediate termination.
